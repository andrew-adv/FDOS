Phase 0 — Weeks 1 & 2 (parallel with Phase 1)

Founder Readiness

Duration 2 Weeks
Runs With Phase 1
Focus Standards & Setup
The goal of Phase 0 is simple: establish that this is real. Not a WhatsApp group with good intentions — a working group with standards. By the end of Week 2, every member should be set up, accounted for, and have demonstrated they can show up consistently.
WEEK 01

Setup & Standards

Get the basics in place. No excuses, no delays.

+

Tasks This Week

Create a dedicated business email address (firstname.lastname@gmail.com or similar — no nicknames)
Share that email with the group so everyone has each other's professional contact
Acquire or designate a business contact number — this is the number you respond to within 24 hours
Submit your weekly availability: the days and hours you can commit to FDOS work each week
Confirm the fixed weekly meeting day and time with the full group
Read: What is an ownership mindset? (see resources below) — come to the Forum ready to discuss

Resources

Book

The War of Art — Steven Pressfield

Read Part 1 only this week (pages 1–65). It's short. It's about resistance — the force that makes people not follow through. Relevant to exactly what this group is working against.

Video

Ownership vs Victimhood Mindset — search "Jocko Willink Extreme Ownership" on YouTube

Watch any clip under 15 minutes. The concept matters more than the specific video.

Tool

Google Workspace — gmail.com

Free. Create your business email here. Also set up Google Calendar and share your availability with the group.

Deliverables

Business email address created and shared with the group
Weekly availability submitted in writing (WhatsApp or wherever the group communicates)
Fixed meeting day and time agreed by the full group

Founder Forum — Week 1 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

What did you complete from this week's task list?

What does "ownership mindset" mean to you — in your own words?

What is one thing that could realistically stop you from showing up consistently to this program?

What do you need from the group to make this work?

WEEK 02

Communication Standards

Define how this group communicates — and hold to it.

+

Tasks This Week

As a group, agree on and write down the communication rules: response time expectation, how to signal you'll be absent, how to raise a problem
Practice writing a professional email — pick any topic, write it, and share it with the group for feedback
Write a summary of last week's Founder Forum meeting — 150 to 200 words. What was discussed, what was decided, what are the open questions
Decide on the group's main workspace platform (Notion, Discord, or other) — bring a 2-minute case for your preference to the Forum

Resources

Video

How to Write a Professional Email — search "professional email writing tips" on YouTube

Any video under 10 minutes. Focus on structure: subject line, opening, body, close.

Article

Meeting Notes 101 — search "how to write meeting minutes" on Google

Read one short article. You'll be writing a meeting summary this week, so understand the format before you start.

Tool

Notion (notion.so) — free plan

If the group chooses Notion, create a free workspace this week and add all members. Start a simple page for team agreements and contact information.

Deliverables

Group communication rules written down and agreed — not just discussed verbally
One professional email written and shared with the group
Week 1 meeting summary written (150–200 words)
Platform decision made and workspace created

Founder Forum — Week 2 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

What did you complete from this week's task list?

What did you find difficult — and did you push through or avoid it?

Present your 2-minute platform recommendation — Notion, Discord, or other. Give one reason for and one against.

What is one professional communication habit you want to build through this program?

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4 (parallel with Phase 0 in Weeks 1–2)

Thinking & Communication

Duration 4 Weeks
Parallel With Phase 0 (Wks 1–2)
Focus Thinking & Expression
By the end of Phase 1, every member should be able to speak clearly in a structured setting, write a professional document, ask questions that extract real information, and think through a problem without jumping to conclusions. These are not soft skills — they are the operating layer everything else runs on.
WEEK 01

How to Think Clearly

First principles, critical thinking, and reasoning from evidence.

+

Tasks This Week

Watch the Farnam Street video on First Principles Thinking (see resources)
Pick one belief you have about AgriVerse or the agriculture space and break it down to first principles — what do you actually know vs what are you assuming?
Write a 200-word reflection: "What is the difference between an assumption and a fact?" Use one example from your own thinking
Prepare a 2-minute verbal explanation of first principles thinking to present at the Forum — as if explaining it to someone who has never heard of it

Resources

Video

First Principles: Elon Musk's Framework for Thinking — Farnam Street (YouTube)

Search "Farnam Street first principles thinking" on YouTube. Watch the full video — it is under 10 minutes.

Video

How to Think for Yourself — search "critical thinking explained simply" on YouTube

Pick any result under 15 minutes that resonates. The concept is more important than the specific presenter.

Article

Mental Models — Farnam Street (fs.blog/mental-models)

Don't read everything. Read the introduction and pick two mental models that interest you. Bring them to the Forum.

Deliverables

200-word written reflection on assumptions vs facts
First principles breakdown of one AgriVerse belief
2-minute Forum presentation on first principles thinking

Founder Forum — Week 1 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

Present your 2-minute explanation of first principles thinking

What assumption about AgriVerse did you break down — and what did you find underneath it?

What two mental models did you read about? Would you use either of them — and when?

What challenged your thinking most this week?

WEEK 02

Speaking & Presenting

Structured delivery, clarity under pressure, active listening.

+

Tasks This Week

Read Chapters 1–3 of Talk Like TED by Carmine Gallo — focus on the concept of delivering one clear idea per talk
Prepare and deliver a 3-minute presentation at the Forum on any topic you know well — could be a hobby, a skill, anything. The topic is not important. The structure is.
After each team member presents, write three sentences: one thing they did well, one thing that could be clearer, one question their talk raised for you
Practise active listening during this week's meetings: take notes, do not interrupt, summarize what you heard before responding

Resources

Book

Talk Like TED — Carmine Gallo

Chapters 1–3 this week. Focus on: one idea, emotional connection, and why stories land harder than facts alone.

Video

The Art of Active Listening — search "active listening skills" on YouTube

Any video under 10 minutes. Then practice it deliberately this week in every group conversation.

Deliverables

3-minute presentation delivered at the Founder Forum
Written feedback given on each other member's presentation (3 sentences each)

Founder Forum — Week 2 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

Deliver your 3-minute presentation

Give your written feedback on each other member's talk

What from Talk Like TED chapters 1–3 surprised you or changed how you think about communication?

Did you practice active listening this week? Where, and what did you notice?

WEEK 03

Professional Writing

Emails, memos, and reports that get read and acted on.

+

Tasks This Week

Write a formal business email to a fictional potential partner for AgriVerse — introduce who you are, what you're building, and why you want to connect
Write a one-page executive summary of FDOS — what it is, why it exists, and what the group is trying to accomplish. Write it as if a stranger will read it
Review each other's email and executive summary — give written feedback focused on: clarity, tone, and whether the purpose is immediately obvious
Rewrite your email or summary based on the feedback you received

Resources

Article

How to Write an Executive Summary — search "executive summary format business" on Google

Read one short article or guide before starting. A good executive summary is half a page to one page. It answers: what, why, and what's next.

Tool

Google Docs — docs.google.com

Write all documents here. Use the "Suggesting" mode to give and receive edits from team members without overwriting each other's work.

Deliverables

One business email — draft and revised version
One executive summary of FDOS — draft and revised version
Written feedback provided to each team member on their documents

Founder Forum — Week 3 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

Read your business email aloud to the group. The group gives verbal feedback before you share the written version.

What was the hardest part of writing clearly this week?

What did the feedback on your writing reveal about how you communicate?

What will you change about how you write after this week?

WEEK 04

Asking Better Questions

The Mom Test method — how to extract truth from a conversation.

+

Tasks This Week

Read The Mom Test by Rob Fitzpatrick — it's short (around 130 pages). Read the whole book this week.
Run one mock customer interview with another team member — one person plays the interviewer, one plays a potential AgriVerse customer. Then swap.
After your mock interview, write a half-page summary: what questions worked, what questions got you opinions instead of facts, what you would ask differently next time
Write a list of 10 questions you would ask a real farmer or agricultural business to understand their problems — then review them against The Mom Test criteria

Resources

Book

The Mom Test — Rob Fitzpatrick

Read the entire book this week. It is 130 pages and written to be read quickly. The core idea: never ask anyone if your idea is good. Ask about their life, their problems, and their behavior instead.

Video

The Mom Test Summary — search "The Mom Test Rob Fitzpatrick summary" on YouTube

Watch a 10-minute summary before you start reading to orient yourself, then read the book fully.

Deliverables

The Mom Test read in full
Mock interview completed and documented (half-page reflection each)
List of 10 customer interview questions — reviewed against Mom Test principles

Founder Forum — Week 4 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

What is the core insight of The Mom Test — in two sentences?

Share one question from your list of 10 that you think is good — and one that violates Mom Test principles. Explain both.

What happened in your mock interview that surprised you?

Are you ready to run a real customer interview? What is still making you uncertain?

Phase 2 — Weeks 5–8 (Assessment Gate falls mid-phase, around Week 6–7)

Business Foundations

Duration 4 Weeks
Gate Week 6–7
Focus How Businesses Work
By the end of Phase 2, every member should understand how businesses actually create and capture value — not in theory, but by being able to look at a real company and explain its model. The Assessment Gate falls around Week 6 or 7. Whether the group passes it or not, Phase 2 still gets completed.
WEEK 05

What Is a Business?

Value creation, delivery, and capture — the fundamentals.

+

Tasks This Week

Watch the Personal MBA core concepts video on value creation (see resources)
Pick one business you use regularly — not AgriVerse. Map out how it creates value, how it delivers that value, and how it captures money from it
Write a 300-word explanation of the business you mapped — as if explaining it to someone who has never heard of it
Try to apply the same framework to AgriVerse as it currently stands: what value would it create, for whom, how would it deliver it, how would it make money?

Resources

Book

The Personal MBA — Josh Kaufman

Read Chapter 1 only this week: "Value Creation." It covers the 12 forms of value and is the clearest explanation of what a business actually does.

Video

How Businesses Make Money — search "value creation business model explained" on YouTube

Any clear video under 15 minutes. Focus on the distinction between creating, delivering, and capturing value.

Deliverables

300-word business breakdown (creation, delivery, capture) for one real company
Initial value map for AgriVerse — rough is fine, it will be refined in Phase 6

Founder Forum — Week 5 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

Present the business you mapped — walk the group through how it creates, delivers, and captures value

Where does AgriVerse sit when you apply the same framework? What is still unclear?

What surprised you most about how the business you studied actually makes money?

What did you complete this week and what did you not complete?

WEEK 06

Business Models

Marketplace, subscription, commission — and which one fits AgriVerse.

+

Tasks This Week

Study three business models in detail: Amazon (retail + marketplace + subscription), Uber (commission marketplace), Jumia (e-commerce in African context)
For each company, write 3–4 sentences answering: who pays, who receives value, how the company takes its cut
As a group discussion: which business model type is closest to what AgriVerse is trying to do? Write down the group's answer with reasoning
Read Chapter 3 of Business Model Generation — the nine building blocks of the Business Model Canvas. You will use this tool fully in Phase 6.

Resources

Video

How Amazon Makes Money — search "Amazon business model explained" on YouTube

Any clear breakdown under 15 minutes. Amazon is worth studying because it runs multiple models at once.

Video

How Uber Makes Money — search "Uber business model" on YouTube

Under 10 minutes. Focus on the two-sided marketplace structure and the commission model.

Book

Business Model Generation — Alexander Osterwalder

Read Chapter 3 this week: the nine building blocks. Don't fill in a canvas yet — just understand what the blocks mean.

Deliverables

3–4 sentence business model breakdown for Amazon, Uber, and Jumia
Written group decision on AgriVerse's likely business model type — with reasoning

Founder Forum — Week 6 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

Walk the group through one of the three companies you studied — what is their model and how does money actually flow?

What business model type does the group think AgriVerse is? Is everyone aligned, or are there different views?

What were the nine building blocks in Business Model Generation — can you name them without looking?

What did you complete this week and what did you not complete?

▲ Assessment Gate — End of Week 6 / Start of Week 7

Before the group continues — answer these honestly

Is this opportunity actually worth pursuing, based on what we now know?

Do we have the right team — has everyone shown up and contributed consistently?

Do we understand the problem well enough to keep going?

Do we have what it takes — the commitment, the time, the willingness to keep going?

Each member answers these individually in writing before the group discusses. The goal is honest individual reflection first, group conversation second. Either outcome — continue, adjust, or stop — is a valid outcome. A dishonest "yes" is the only wrong answer.

WEEK 07

Economics for Founders

Supply, demand, incentives, competition — the forces your business will operate inside.

+

Tasks This Week

Read the first 3 chapters of Economics in One Lesson by Henry Hazlitt — it is available as a free PDF online
Apply what you learn to the AgriVerse context: who are the buyers, who are the sellers, what determines price, who else is competing for the same customer?
Write a 200-word analysis: what economic forces will most affect AgriVerse? Think about things like: price sensitivity of farmers, existing competition, government policy, seasonal demand
Identify one competitor — direct or indirect — and write one paragraph on how they are positioned in the market

Resources

Book

Economics in One Lesson — Henry Hazlitt (free PDF)

Search "Economics in One Lesson PDF free download." Read Chapters 1–3. This book is 80 years old and still one of the clearest introductions to economic thinking for non-economists.

Video

Supply and Demand Explained — search "supply and demand explained simply" on YouTube

Any clear explanation under 10 minutes. Focus on what shifts supply and demand and what that means for price.

Deliverables

200-word economic analysis of AgriVerse's environment
One-paragraph competitor positioning writeup

Founder Forum — Week 7 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

What is the single most important economic force that will affect AgriVerse — and why?

Present your competitor analysis — who are they, how are they positioned, and what does that mean for us?

What from the Hazlitt reading changed or sharpened how you think about markets?

What did you complete this week and what did you not complete?

WEEK 08

Startup Fundamentals

MVP, product-market fit, and the Lean Startup method.

+

Tasks This Week

Read Chapters 1–4 of The Lean Startup by Eric Ries — focus on the Build-Measure-Learn loop and the definition of an MVP
In your own words, write a 150-word answer to: "What is an MVP, and what is it not?"
As a group, sketch what the simplest possible version of AgriVerse would look like — the one that could be tested with real people in under 4 weeks with no budget. Write it up.
Prepare a Phase 2 summary to present at the Forum: what the group now understands about business that it didn't understand 4 weeks ago

Resources

Book

The Lean Startup — Eric Ries

Read Chapters 1–4 this week. The key concept: validated learning over assumptions. An MVP is not a cheap product — it is a specific experiment designed to test one assumption.

Video

What is an MVP? — search "minimum viable product explained" on YouTube

Watch before you read so the book's examples land more clearly. Under 10 minutes.

Video

What is Product Market Fit? — search "product market fit explained founders" on YouTube

Any clear video under 12 minutes. This concept will become very relevant in Phase 3 when you start validating.

Deliverables

150-word written answer: "What is an MVP and what is it not?"
Group sketch of the simplest testable version of AgriVerse — written up clearly
Phase 2 summary: what the group now understands about business that it didn't 4 weeks ago

Founder Forum — Week 8 Prompts

Each member answers these at the weekly meeting

What is an MVP — and what would AgriVerse's MVP look like at this stage?

Present your Phase 2 summary: what do you understand now that you didn't 4 weeks ago?

How has your view of AgriVerse as a business changed over the last 8 weeks?

Are you ready for Phase 3? What are you most uncertain about going into Research & Validation?
